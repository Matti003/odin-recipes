Tacos

Description

Tacos are a traditional Mexican dish consisting of a corn or wheat tortilla filled with a variety of ingredients, such as seasoned meat, cheese, lettuce, tomatoes, beans, salsa, avocado, and sour cream. Tacos can be made with a variety of proteins, including beef, chicken, pork, and fish, and can be seasoned with spices such as cumin, chili powder, and paprika. The tortilla is typically folded in half around the filling and eaten by hand. Tacos are a popular street food in Mexico and have become a staple of Mexican cuisine around the world, with many variations and regional styles. They are often served with rice, beans, and salsa on the side and are a simple, tasty, and convenient meal that can be enjoyed any time of day.

ingredients

  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 8 corn tortillas
  • Shredded lettuce
  • Diced tomatoes
  • Shredded cheese
  • Salsa
  • Sour cream

Instructions

  1. In a large skillet, brown the ground beef over medium-high heat until fully cooked, about 8-10 minutes. Drain any excess fat.
  2. Stir in the chili powder, cumin, garlic powder, onion powder, oregano, salt, and pepper into the cooked beef and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  3. Warm the tortillas in the oven, on a griddle, or in a dry skillet until soft and pliable.
  4. To assemble the tacos, place a spoonful of the beef mixture onto a warm tortilla, followed by a handful of shredded lettuce, diced tomatoes, shredded cheese, salsa, and a dollop of sour cream.
  5. Fold the tortilla in half around the filling and serve.
